Reading Starlight

Build the decoder ring yourself. Make a spectrum, excite elements across the periodic table, flip emission into absorption at the same wavelengths, compare causes, then read a real star.

1. The Experiment
Watch the light travel through the apparatus.
Absorption experiment
This is the physical setup: source, slit, prism, and detector. Switch between the hot-gas and cool-gas versions of the experiment and change the prism dispersion to see the separation grow or shrink.
1.00x
Short wavelengths bend more in the prism, long wavelengths bend less. In hot-gas mode the detector records bright emission lines. In cool-gas mode those same wavelengths become dark absorption lines.
What To Notice
The prism matters because it turns wavelength into position. The detector does not “know colors.” It records where different wavelengths land after the prism splits them. That is why spectral lines can be photographed and measured.
