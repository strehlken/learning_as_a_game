Sellmeier equation

n²(λ) = 1 + Σ Bᵢλ²/(λ²−Cᵢ). Published coefficients from the Schott glass catalog give n(λ) to 5+ decimal places across the visible spectrum.

Snell's law × 2

n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂ at entry and exit. Higher n(λ) for short wavelengths → violet bends more than red. Total deviation δ = θᵢ + θₒ − A.

Inside the glass

Frequency constant; wavelength contracts to λ/n(λ); phase velocity drops to c/n(λ). The wave crests visibly compress and slow inside the prism.
