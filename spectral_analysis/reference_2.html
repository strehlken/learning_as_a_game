Wave Propagation

Each dot on the sine curve carries its value and moves at whatever speed the medium has at its current position. The wavelength compression inside the glass is not set — it emerges from the dots bunching up when they slow down.

How it works: A source emits points at a fixed frequency. Each point is born with a sine value — sin(ω · t_birth) — that it keeps forever. It then moves through the scene at local speed: c in air, c/n in glass. When a point enters the glass, it slows down. The point behind it is still in air, still fast, closing the gap. By the time both are inside, they're closer together than they were in air — by exactly the factor 1/n. That's the wavelength contraction. It was not computed. It happened because the back point caught up to the front point. The direction change at each prism face is Snell's law applied to the velocity vector, once, at the moment of crossing. After that the point just keeps going straight at the new speed.
